1Executive Summary

WellnessConnect is a digital platform designed to address the trust deficit in the European home-care market by connecting users with rigorously verified healthcare and wellness professionals. Launching initially in Italy, a market characterized by significant demographic pressures and healthcare system fragmentation, the platform offers a transparent, secure, and efficient solution for accessing at-home services.

The core value proposition is centered on a multi-stage verification protocol for all listed professionals, solving the primary market pain point of provider trustworthiness. The business model combines service commissions with future subscription and B2B revenue streams. We project reaching profitability within four years, targeting 0.75% of Italy's estimated €16 billion home healthcare market within five years, which represents a Gross Service Value (GSV) of €120 million.

To empower families with trusted, accessible, and personalized home-care and wellness services through a secure, user-centric technology platform. This aligns with UN Sustainable Development Goal 3 (Good Health and Well-being) and Goal 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth).

PESTEL Analysis

This analysis identifies the key macro-environmental factors driving the demand for a new home-care delivery model.

  • Political: EU and national governments, including Italy via its National Recovery and Resilience Plan (NRRP), are promoting digital health and "aging in place" policies to alleviate pressure on public healthcare systems (Servizio Sanitario Nazionale - SSN).
  • Economic: Rising healthcare expenditures and strained public budgets create a favorable environment for cost-effective private solutions. While wellness spending may be sensitive to economic downturns, essential home-care is largely recession-resilient.
  • Social: Europe's aging demographic is the primary market driver. According to Eurostat, the median age in Italy reached 48.4 years in 2023, one of the highest in the EU. This creates sustained demand for elderly care, chronic disease management, and post-operative support.
  • Technological: Increased digital literacy among all age groups and the proliferation of mobile technology make a platform-based model viable and expected by consumers. The adoption of telehealth and remote monitoring tools is accelerating.
  • Environmental: A focus on reducing travel-related carbon footprints supports the logic of localized, at-home service delivery over centralized, facility-based care.
  • Legal: The regulatory landscape is complex, governed by the EU's GDPR for data privacy and the Medical Device Regulation (MDR) for certain health technologies. This creates high barriers to entry but rewards compliant, well-structured platforms.

Competitive Landscape & The Competitive Gap

The market is fragmented among traditional, inefficient, or low-trust alternatives, leaving a clear gap for a technology-enabled leader focused on verification.

The Italian market lacks a dominant, trusted digital platform for home care. The competitive landscape includes:

  • Digital-First Platforms: Companies like Santagostino and EpiCura offer a range of health services, including some home visits, but operate primarily through a centralized, clinic-based model. Their digitalization is high, but their focus is not exclusively on a verified, decentralized home-care network.
  • Assistance-Focused Services: Players like UGO and PrivatoAssistenza focus on non-medical assistance and elderly support. They often rely on franchise or agency models with varying levels of digitalization and inconsistent verification standards.
  • Informal Market: The largest competitor is the informal economy of undeclared workers, which offers low cost but carries significant risks regarding quality, safety, and legality.

Labor Compliance and Professional Onboarding

A critical operational and legal decision is the employment classification of our professionals, which directly impacts compliance, scalability, and our value proposition.

In the Italian market, professionals can operate either as employees or as independent freelancers (possessori di Partita IVA). WellnessConnect will operate a pure marketplace model, onboarding professionals exclusively as independent freelancers. This strategic choice is based on several factors:

  • Scalability: A freelancer model allows for rapid scaling of the professional network without the significant administrative and financial burden of direct employment.
  • Flexibility: It offers professionals autonomy over their schedules and client selection, a key factor in attracting top-tier talent seeking to supplement their primary income or manage their own practice.
  • Legal Clarity: By acting as a technology intermediary (marketplace) rather than an employer agency, we maintain a clear legal and operational model, reducing complexities related to labor contracts, payroll, and social security contributions (INPS).

Our verification process will therefore include validation of a professional's "Partita IVA" status and their compliance with Italian fiscal and insurance requirements for freelancers, reinforcing our commitment to a fully formal and transparent ecosystem.

Break-Even Point (BEP) Analysis

The BEP analysis determines the point at which total revenues equal total costs, providing a key indicator of financial viability.

The break-even point is a critical milestone where the business is no longer generating a loss. It is calculated as: BEP (in Revenue) = Fixed Costs / (1 - (Variable Costs / Revenue)). Based on our projections, fixed costs primarily consist of salaries and platform maintenance, while variable costs are linked to transaction processing and performance marketing. We project that WellnessConnect will reach its break-even point in early Year 4, as revenue growth and margin improvements outpace the growth in fixed operating expenses.
